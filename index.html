  • Dance

    2017

    The Magic of Tihar: Dancing through the Streets

    It all started with a group of friends who invited me to join them for a deusi and bhailo performance during the festival of Tihar. I had never been much of a dancer and had always avoided any kind of social gatherings where dancing was involved. But this time, I decided to give it a try.
    Deusi and Bhailo are traditional folk dances that are performed during the Tihar festival in Nepal. The dances are performed by groups of men and women who go from house to house singing and dancing to the beats of traditional instruments. The songs usually revolve around the worship of the Hindu goddess Laxmi and the celebration of the festival.
    As I joined my friends on the streets, I was immediately struck by the vibrant energy and lively atmosphere. The music was upbeat and catchy, and the dancers were all dressed in traditional Nepali attire. I found myself tapping my feet to the beat and before I knew it, I was dancing along with my friends.
    I must admit, I was a bit nervous at first. I felt self-conscious and wondered if I was doing the dance steps correctly. But as I got more into the rhythm, I let go of my inhibitions and just enjoyed the moment.
    As we went from house to house, I couldn't help but notice the warm welcome we received from the hosts. They would offer us sweets and snacks, and we would exchange pleasantries before moving on to the next house. This sense of community and togetherness is what makes the deusi and bhailo tradition so special.
    The experience was truly enlightening for me. I had never felt so alive and free. Dancing had always been something that I had avoided, but now I realize how much I had been missing out on. I am grateful to my friends for introducing me to this beautiful tradition and I look forward to participating in it every year.
    In conclusion, the traditional folk dances of deusi and bhailo are not just about the dancing itself but also about the celebration of culture, community and tradition. It was an experience that I will cherish forever, and I encourage everyone to give it a try and experience the joy and energy of these dances.
